Counterfeiting 101

Counterfeiting vs. Piracy
Counterfeiting refers to the copying of physical products and brands. Piracy regards to the illegal reproduction of intellectual, content products, such as music, movies, software, or print
Deceptive vs. Non-Deceptive Counterfeiting
Deceptive counterfeits fool buyers into purchasing fake goods, the buyers believing it is an original product. Buyers purchasing nondeceptive counterfeits are willing accomplices as they intentionally buy a counterfeit good.
Laissez-Faire Attitude vs. Proactive Approach
Some companies remain passive, often due to missing market intelligence. In many cases business are the last to nd out about the depth of the problem. Whilst others decide the proactive approach will not just save revenue but create consumer condence.
Internal vs. External Attacks
Internal attacks are committed by people or entities directly or indirectly linked with the legitimate manufacturer (staff, subcontractor, supplier, etc.), external attacks by people or entities not linked. There is a high percentage of internal attack partners and if a currently licensed producer simultaneously sells counterfeits, it is called out-the-backdoor counterfeiting.
